# Framing: Disability / Neurodivergent Founder

**For:** Disability-specific grants, DEI narratives, neurodivergent founder funding
**Identity positions:** Community Practitioner, Creative Technologist

## Core Argument

Disability-specific funding is among the least competitive grant categories — fewer applicants per dollar than general startup grants. Most disabled founders don't know these programs exist, and most non-disabled founders don't realize that conditions like ADHD, anxiety disorders, and chronic illness often qualify.

The framing that opens doors: "I'm a neurodivergent founder building AI infrastructure" — identity-first, then the work. This positions the application in the least competitive pool while accessing the same caliber of funding.

## Key Programs

- **2Gether-International:** First-ever VC fund specifically for founders with disabilities, raising $5M in philanthropic capital
- **Comcast:** $1.5M through 2025 to equip disabled startup founders
- **FedEx Entrepreneur Fund:** 10 grants of $30,000 for entrepreneurs with disabilities
- **PASS Program:** Plan to Achieve Self-Support allows setting aside money for business expenses without affecting SSI benefits
- **169+ Active Diversity Grants:** Median $21,250, average $231,520 — 30.6% of deadlines fall in Q2

## Eligibility Notes

Neurodivergent conditions (ADHD, autism spectrum, anxiety disorders) and chronic illness frequently qualify for disability-specific funding. Self-identification is standard practice for most programs — medical documentation requirements vary.

## Application Template

When applications require DEI narrative:
1. Lead with identity positioning (neurodivergent founder)
2. Connect to the work (systematic thinking → system architecture)
3. Frame the ORGANVM system as evidence: governance structure, documentation rigor, and sustained independent production demonstrate how neurodivergent cognitive patterns become engineering advantages at scale
4. Cite the numbers: 113 repositories, 23,470 tests, 33 sprints — sustained output that reframes "disability" as different, not deficient
