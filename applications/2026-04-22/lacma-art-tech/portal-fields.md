### 1. Project Description

I am applying for the Art + Technology Lab grant to share and expand **ORGANVM: The Eight-Organ Metasystem**. ORGANVM is an autonomous work of "Infrastructure Art" encompassing 113 repositories, 21,160 code files, and ~810K+ words of documentation, all coordinated across 8 independent GitHub organizations. It is an exploration of how the tools of institutional-scale engineering can be reclaimed as a primary medium for artistic expression.

My work is built on the "Cathedral → Storefront" philosophy: maintaining a deep, recursive "Cathedral" of systemic logic while providing high-signal, scannable "Storefront" entry points for human-agent collaboration.

I propose to use the Lab's resources to address three core systemic inquiries:

1. **The Aesthetics of Governance:** ORGANVM is governed by a formal promotion state machine and a validated dependency graph with 43 edges. I treat the CI/CD pipeline not as a utility, but as a choreographic space. Every commit is a performance, validated by a corpus of 2,349+ automated tests that serve as aesthetic sensors for correctness and integrity.

2. **Steerable AI Orchestration:** I utilize 32 specialized AI agent definitions as compositional instruments to drive solo production at institutional scale. This "AI-conductor" methodology, documented across 48 published essays (~142K words), shifts the focus from AI as a generative tool to AI as a steerable member of a collaborative team. I am eager to explore how LACMA's technology partners might provide access to frontier models or specialized hardware to push the boundaries of this orchestration.

3. **Infrastructure as Public Model:** My work responds to the opacity of modern technical systems by offering a "Cathedral" built entirely on human-readable markdown and validated schemas. I aim to build the "operating system" for a more transparent digital future—one where technical integrity serves as a form of verifiable trust between the artist and the public.

LACMA's history of fostering radical collaborations between artists and industry is the ideal environment for ORGANVM. I am eager to work with the Lab's partners to explore how we might build more resilient, autonomous, and beautiful cultural infrastructures for the AI era.

### 2. Artist Statement

I build creative systems — the governance IS the artwork.

My practice centers on the eight-organ system: a documented infrastructure coordinating theory, generative art, commercial products, governance, public process, community, and marketing across 113 repositories. I don't produce individual artworks. I produce the *systems* that generate, coordinate, and sustain creative work — and the visible record of how those systems are built is itself the primary creative output.

The process of creation is the product. The 48 essays I've published aren't reflections on the work; they ARE the work — the creative process rendered into prose in real time. The governance rules — registry design, dependency graphs, promotion pipelines — aren't bureaucratic overhead; they're generative constraints, the way a composer's harmonic rules shape what melodies can emerge.

I work in the tradition of solo producers who became orchestras: Brian Eno treating the studio as a compositional instrument, Trent Reznor building entire albums alone because nobody else would commit at the necessary intensity. I use AI tools the way these practitioners used their technologies — as instruments for realizing an architectural vision, not as replacements for creative judgment.

The eight-organ system is simultaneously: (a) working infrastructure that produces creative work, (b) a documented methodology for solo production at institutional scale, (c) an argument that the process of creation — made visible and governable — is itself the most interesting thing an artist can produce, and (d) a reusable model other practitioners can learn from.
