# Cover Letter: Creative Capital 2027

Dear Creative Capital,

I am applying for the 2027 Open Call with ORGANVM, a living creative system that treats governance infrastructure as an artistic medium.

## The Project

ORGANVM coordinates 117 repositories across 8 GitHub organizations — spanning theory, generative art, commercial products, orchestration, public discourse, community, and distribution. A machine-readable registry serves as its constitution. Automated dependency validation enforces that theory feeds art, art feeds commerce — never the reverse. A promotion state machine governs how work evolves. The system is simultaneously working infrastructure and an argument that the most interesting thing an artist can produce is a visible, self-governing creative process.

This is not a portfolio of finished objects. It is a single organism whose creative output is the process itself, made legible.

## Why This Is Art

The eight-organ system draws from Julian Oliver's critical engineering, Nicky Case's Explorable Explanations, and Hundred Rabbits' radical transparency — practitioners who treat protocols and tool-making as primary creative outputs. My contribution is scale: a single practitioner building and governing a system of institutional complexity, documented through 49 published essays and 741,000 words of public process.

The governance structures — constitutional constraints, dependency graphs, promotion pipelines — are generative in the way a composer's harmonic rules are generative. They don't restrict; they shape what can emerge. The Studium Generale ORGANVM, the system's internal university, even evaluates its own knowledge production through multi-model consensus — an institution that certifies its own scholarship through statistical agreement rather than individual judgment.

## Why Creative Capital

Creative Capital supports artists at the intersection of ambition and risk. ORGANVM is both: an artwork that requires infrastructure thinking, built by a single practitioner using AI tools as compositional instruments — the way Brian Eno used the recording studio. The AI-conductor model (human directs architecture, AI generates volume, human reviews) enabled one person to produce at institutional scale. This methodology is documented transparently, including failures and course corrections.

The practical evidence: 23,470 verified tests across 20 repositories. 104 CI/CD pipelines. Zero dependency violations across 50 validated edges. 33 named development sprints. An MFA in Creative Writing grounds the entire practice in formal creative education. 11 years of college instruction across 8+ institutions and 2,000+ students provides the pedagogical instinct to make complex systems legible.

## What the Funding Enables

Creative Capital support would fund three initiatives:

1. The Studium Generale ORGANVM — completing the internal university's defense system, running formal evaluations of the two inaugural dissertations, and publishing the methodology as an arXiv preprint demonstrating that AI-mediated peer review can achieve substantial inter-rater agreement.

2. Public exhibitions — translating the system's governance graphs, dependency flows, and promotion state machines into visual and interactive installations that make infrastructure aesthetics tangible to non-technical audiences.

3. Community infrastructure — hosting the first salons and reading groups through ORGAN-VI (Koinonia), opening the system's methodology to collaborative expansion beyond solo production.

The end-state is not a solo operation. It is a multi-media production studio — but one that started from the most constrained possible position to prove that the architecture works before scaling.
