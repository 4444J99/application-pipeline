## How do you use AI in your day-to-day work? What are some of the underappreciated benefits and/or pain points of your favorite AI tools?

I build with Claude Code daily — 113 repositories governed by CLAUDE.md context files that I auto-generate from Jinja2 templates bound to live registry data. AI generates the volume; human judgment governs what gets promoted through a 4-state machine (LOCAL → CANDIDATE → PUBLIC_PROCESS → GRADUATED). Every AI output passes through the same quality gates as human work.

Underappreciated benefit: AI as a testing multiplier. I went from 1,200 to 23,470 tests in 3 months — not by asking AI to write tests, but by using it to generate the coverage matrix and identify untested state transitions. The AI finds the gaps; I decide what constitutes a valid assertion.

Biggest pain point: context window decay in long sessions. After 45 minutes of complex refactoring, every AI tool starts contradicting its own earlier decisions. The workaround is structured session handoff — I write cross-agent context documents that compress 2 hours of work into 500 words of state. That is an infrastructure problem, not a model problem, and it is exactly the kind of thing Perplexity's architecture is positioned to solve.
