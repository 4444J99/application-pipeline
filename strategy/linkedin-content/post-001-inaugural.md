## Testament Audit (retroactive)

### Violations:
- **Art. II (Cascading Causation):** AND_THEN structure throughout. "The system: X. Y. Z." is a list, not an argument. No sentence causes the next.
- **Art. III (Triple Layer):** Pure ethos (credentials). No pathos (felt truth). No logos (argument). Reads as a resume, not a post.
- **Art. X (Opening Architecture):** No title/subtitle/precis. No hook. Opens with a claim ("I build...") not a tension.
- **Art. XI (Paragraph Discipline):** Single block of text. No subheadings. No paragraph clustering. No story spine.
- **Art. XII (Charged Language):** "Key projects I'm sharing publicly" is throat-clearing. "The bridge between humanities and engineering isn't a gap — it's a discipline" is the only charged sentence — and it's buried at the bottom.
- **Art. XIII (Power Position):** Final words: "...merge," "...code," "...generation," "...Writing," "...discipline." Only "discipline" has weight. The heartbeat is flat until the last beat.
- **Art. V (Collision Geometry):** No collision. Three projects listed but no bridge element connecting them. They're parallel, never converging.
- **Art. I (Knowledge Imperative):** Creates zero new knowledge. Every claim is verifiable but none is surprising. The reader learns WHAT exists but not WHY it matters or WHAT it means.

### What works:
- "The bridge between humanities and engineering isn't a gap — it's a discipline" — this is the post's only thesis-grade sentence. It should be the OPENING, not the closing.
- The numbers are real and specific (not rounded vanity metrics).
- narratological-algorithmic-lenses is genuinely surprising content — 14 theories × 92 algorithms — but it's buried in a list.

### Recommended revision direction:
Open with the bridge sentence as the hook. The tension: most people see humanities and engineering as separate. The post argues they're the same discipline. The 113 repos, the narratological algorithms, the governance infrastructure — these are evidence for that argument, not items in a catalog. The revision should argue, not list.
