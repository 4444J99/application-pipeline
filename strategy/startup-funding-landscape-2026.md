## 2. Angel Investing

### Angel Group Activity

- **ACA membership**: 14,000+ accredited investors across 250+ angel groups, platforms, and family offices
- **2025 report finding**: Deal volume softened and exits slowed, but ACA members maintained commitments to earliest-stage investing
- **Median angel group investment per deal**: $88K at <$2.5M valuations (down 17% since 2022); $2.5-$5M range decreased 24% vs 2022 peak

### Syndicate Activity

- **Average syndicate check size**: $100K-$350K per SPV (up to $1M per deal; average closer to $300K)
- **AngelList syndicate allocations**: $200K-$300K average; range observed $50K-$400K
- **Individual minimums**: $1,000-$5,000 for accredited investors on AngelList
- **Top syndicate platforms (2025-2026)**: AngelList, AngelList India, SyndicateRoom, FundersClub, Republic

### Trend: Angel Capital Under Pressure

- Softening deal flow at earliest stages
- Fewer exits creating longer hold periods
- Increasing competition from micro-VCs and rolling funds
- Angels concentrating capital in fewer, higher-conviction bets

## 3. Pre-Seed / Seed Dynamics

### Traction Metrics VCs Expect (2026)

| Stage | Funding Range | What Investors Want |
|-------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Pre-Seed** | $150K-$1M | Functional MVP, early user feedback, preliminary validation. 1-2 founders. |
| **Seed** | $2M-$4M (median $3.1M) | $300K-$500K ARR, MoM traction, unit economics clarity, LTV/CAC ≥ 3:1, burn multiple 1-2x |
| **Series A** | $10M-$20M (median $12M) | $1M-$3M ARR minimum (some say $2M-$5M), 15-25% MoM growth, low churn, predictable growth engine |
| **Series B** | $20M-$60M (median $38M) | $5M-$10M ARR, NRR >100%, repeatable processes, not founder-dependent |

### Key Benchmark Shifts

- **"Pitch deck + prototype" seed rounds are over** — except for repeat founders and exceptional AI teams
- AI startups command a **1.3x premium** on median seed deal size ($4.6M vs $3.1M overall)
- Equity dilution at seed: consistent median of **20%** across last 7 quarters (15-20% range)
- Less than **40%** of seed-funded startups reach Series A
- Only **36%** of 2020-2021 Series A companies subsequently raised Series B

## 5. Geographic Trends

### Capital Concentration

- **U.S. market share**: $274B in 2025 (64% of global funding) — significant jump from 56% in 2024 and 47-48% average in 2019-2023
- **San Francisco Bay Area**: $111.7B through Q3 2025 alone — nearly 8x New York's total
- **AI concentration**: SF Bay Area raised $122B of the $159B in U.S. AI funding (76%+)
- **Bay Area tech density**: 49% of all Big Tech engineers, 27% of startup engineers

### Why Silicon Valley Dominates Even More Now

1. World's top VC firms managing $200B+ in assets concentrated there
2. 6,263 startups per 100K people — unmatched ecosystem density
3. Stanford/UC Berkeley pipeline + Big Tech alumni
4. 50%+ of global AI funding flows through SF
5. 268 unicorns creating reinvestment cycles

### Emerging Hubs (But Still Far Behind)

- **New York**: Second but roughly 1/8 of SF volume
- **Austin, Miami, Los Angeles, Seattle**: Growing but not approaching Bay Area scale
- **U.S. Heartland**: Alumni Ventures invested ~$150M in heartland startups
- **Europe**: Deep Tech attracted nearly 1/3 of European VC funding in 2024; AI led European funding for the first time in 2025
- **Middle East**: Record $1.2B in Q3 2025 (Saudi Vision 2030 effects)
- **Latin America**: 39 unicorns (nearly 3x the 2020 count)

### Remote-First vs Hub Preference

Remote work created some geographic diversification post-2020, but venture dollars have **re-concentrated** in established hubs — particularly SF — driven by AI companies requiring proximity to talent, compute infrastructure, and model labs.

## 7. Fundraising Timeline

### How Long Rounds Take to Close (2025-2026)

| Metric | Duration | Trend |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **Seed round closing** | ~142 days median (up from 68 days in 2021) | 2x longer than boom era |
| **General round closing** | ~4 months / 20 weeks | Stable but extended |
| **Seed → Series A interval** | 616 days / ~20 months (Q2 2025) | 2+ months longer than 2023 |
| **Series A → Series B interval** | 28 months median / 31 months average (2024) | Longest since 2012 |
| **Overall median between rounds** | 696 days / ~23 months | +5% YoY |

### Key Takeaways

- What used to be a 3-week seed sprint has become a **3-6 month marathon**
- Investors are more selective: fewer deals, deeper diligence
- Bridge rounds have grown as companies extend runway between primary rounds
- Best practice for 2026: plan for minimum 4-5 month fundraise; start 6+ months before cash runs out

### Notable Speed Exceptions

- **xAI**: Closed $6B Series B just 6 months after prior round
- **Figure** (AI robotics): Series B in <1 year after Series A
- Speed correlates heavily with AI sector + exceptional traction

## 8. Down Rounds and Market Corrections

### Down Round Data

- **Q1 2025**: 19% of all new rounds on Carta were down rounds
- **Q3 2025**: 17% down-round rate — lowest quarterly rate in nearly 3 years
- **Historical context**: Down rounds exceeded 20% in 7 of 8 quarters from Q2 2023 through Q1 2025
- **Trend**: Declining as 2021/2022 vintage companies work through repricing

### IPO Repricing

- Two-thirds of 2025 unicorn IPOs were priced below their last private valuation
- Median IPO valuation relative to last VC valuation: **0.9x**
- However, many subsequently traded up substantially post-listing — signaling real demand at realistic prices
- IPO volumes grew 20%, proceeds grew 84% in the trailing 12 months

### Contraction Signals

1. **Deal count declining**: Fewer than 1,500 deals under $100M funded in Q4 2025 for the entire U.S.
2. **Capital concentration extreme**: 0.05% of deals capturing 50% of dollars
3. **New VC firm fundraising**: "Disastrous climate" for emerging managers (Bloomberg)
4. **Series A squeeze**: Series A funding slid in Q2 2025 (Carta)
5. **Non-AI SaaS**: Facing flat-to-slightly-up rounds with tighter terms

### Correction Nuance

The market is **bifurcated**, not uniformly contracting:
- AI-native companies: Rising valuations, faster closes, abundant capital
- Everything else: Slower fundraising, valuation discipline, fundamentals-focused investors
- Secondary markets growing to $160B+ in 2024, projected $210B+ in 2025 — providing alternative liquidity
- M&A surging: 40% YoY volume increase in Q3 2025; Google/Wiz $32B as landmark

## Key Takeaways for March 2026

1. **AI is the market.** 48-50% of all venture dollars go to AI. Non-AI companies face a fundamentally different (and harder) fundraising environment.
2. **Capital concentration is extreme.** 0.05% of deals capture 50% of dollars. The median startup experience is far worse than the aggregate numbers suggest.
3. **Fundraising takes much longer.** Seed rounds now take 4-5 months; between-round intervals average 23 months. Plan accordingly.
4. **Valuations are bifurcated.** AI companies see record valuations; non-AI companies face discipline and down-round risk (still 17% of rounds).
5. **Geography is re-concentrating.** U.S. share jumped from 56% to 64%; SF Bay Area dominates even more with AI.
6. **Exits are finally happening.** IPOs, M&A ($32B Wiz), and secondary markets ($160B+) are providing overdue liquidity.
7. **Angel investing is under pressure.** Check sizes declining, exits slower, competition from micro-VCs.
8. **Defense tech and robotics are rising.** a16z allocated $1.12B specifically; government procurement accelerating.
