## 1. Revenue-Based Financing

Non-dilutive capital advanced against recurring revenue. The RBF market is forecasted to exceed $42 billion by 2027. Best for SaaS/subscription businesses with $10K+ MRR.

### Pipe

- **URL**: [https://pipe.com](https://pipe.com)
- **Source**: [Pipe Review & Alternatives (ECL, 2026)](https://www.ecaplabs.com/blogs/pipe-review-alternatives)
- **Amount**: $25,000 to $100M per transaction
- **Eligibility**: Companies with recurring revenue (SaaS, D2C subscriptions, media, services). Headquartered in U.S. or UK. Minimum ~$10K MRR recommended.
- **Key Terms**: Fixed trading fee up to 1% per party. Repayment terms 6-12 months. Marketplace model (investors bid on revenue contracts). Approval in as little as 2 hours.
- **Note**: Pipe operates as a marketplace, not a direct lender. Investors compete on pricing.

### Capchase

- **URL**: [https://www.capchase.com](https://www.capchase.com)
- **Source**: [Capchase Review (Founderpath)](https://founderpath.com/blog/capchase-review); [Revenue-Based Financing Guide (Float Finance, 2026)](https://www.floatfinance.com/blog-post/revenue-based-financing-the-complete-2026-guide-for-saas-cfos-ceos)
- **Amount**: $25,000 to $10M per transaction; 20-70% of future ARR
- **Eligibility**: U.S. companies with $1M+ ARR (some products available at $100K ARR). Company must be 8+ months old.
- **Key Terms**: Fee percentage assigned per loan (repay 5-15% of monthly revenue). Repayment terms 3-12 months. No pitch decks needed; connects banking/billing data for algorithmic underwriting. Offers in 24-48 hours. Direct lender (own balance sheet).

### Clearco

- **URL**: [https://www.clear.co](https://www.clear.co)
- **Source**: [Clearco Review 2026 (United Capital Source)](https://www.unitedcapitalsource.com/business-loans/lender-reviews/clearbanc-review/)
- **Amount**: $10K+ (custom amounts based on revenue)
- **Eligibility**: U.S.-incorporated businesses with 12+ months of recurring revenue generating $100K+ USD/month. D2C ecommerce and/or SaaS.
- **Key Terms**: Two models -- Rolling Funding (ongoing access, repay as you earn) and Invoice Funding (vendor payments). Fees 6-12.5% depending on term (4, 5, or 6 months). Weekly repayments 1-20% of monthly revenue. No personal guarantees, no blanket liens. Approved in ~24 hours. Rebuilt model in Sept 2025 for ecommerce rhythms.

**Overall RBF Assessment**: These three dominate the market but address different niches. Pipe is a marketplace (best pricing competition), Capchase is a direct lender (best for SaaS with $1M+ ARR), and Clearco targets ecommerce/D2C. All are non-dilutive. The key risk is cash-flow compression during down months since repayments are revenue-linked.

**Further Reading**: [13 Best Revenue-Based Financing Companies in 2026 (Startup Savant)](https://startupsavant.com/startup-finance/best-revenue-based-financing-companies); [Top 20 RBF Companies (The CFO Club)](https://thecfoclub.com/strategic-growth-investments/revenue-based-financing-companies/)

## 2. Crowdfunding

### Rewards-Based: Kickstarter & Indiegogo

- **Kickstarter URL**: [https://www.kickstarter.com](https://www.kickstarter.com)
- **Indiegogo URL**: [https://www.indiegogo.com](https://www.indiegogo.com)
- **Source**: [Indiegogo vs Kickstarter 2026 (LaunchBoom)](https://www.launchboom.com/crowdfunding-guides/indiegogo-vs-kickstarter-whats-the-difference); [10 Best Crowdfunding Sites 2026 (Shopify)](https://www.shopify.com/blog/crowdfunding-sites)
- **Amount**: No cap (top campaigns raise $1M+). Average successful tech campaign: $20K-$100K.
- **Eligibility**: Anyone with a product concept. Kickstarter is all-or-nothing (must hit goal). Indiegogo offers flexible funding.
- **Key Terms**: Kickstarter takes 5% platform fee + 3-5% payment processing. Indiegogo takes 5% (or 9% for InDemand). Technology campaigns saw 15% increase in average funds raised in 2025-2026.
- **2026 Trends**: Backers are more sophisticated, behaving like early investors. Pre-launch community building is mandatory. Post-campaign sales (InDemand) increasingly important. Indiegogo stronger for tech gadgets; Kickstarter stronger for creative/design.

### Equity Crowdfunding: Republic, Wefunder, StartEngine

- **Republic URL**: [https://republic.com](https://republic.com)
- **Wefunder URL**: [https://wefunder.com](https://wefunder.com)
- **StartEngine URL**: [https://www.startengine.com](https://www.startengine.com)
- **Source**: [Best Reg CF Platforms 2026 (Angel School)](https://www.angelschool.vc/blog/best-reg-cf-crowdfunding-platforms-for-investments); [Funding Report February 2026 (Kingscrowd)](https://kingscrowd.com/funding-report-for-february-2026/); [2025 Investment Crowdfunding Annual Report (Kingscrowd)](https://kingscrowd.com/2025-investment-crowdfunding-annual-report/)
- **Amount**: Up to $5M per 12-month period under Regulation CF (SEC limit).
- **Eligibility**: Any U.S. company. Must file Form C with SEC. Financial statements required (audited above $618K raised). Statements valid 120 days after fiscal year end.
- **Key Terms**: Platforms take 6-10% of funds raised. Wefunder raised $109M in 2025; StartEngine $89M; Republic $20M. StartEngine surpassed $1.5B cumulative by mid-2025. Increasingly treated as a go-to-market channel, not just fundraising.
- **Regulation CF Updates**: SEC allows $5M annual cap. Financial statements expire April 2026 for 2023-2024 filings. Transactions must occur through SEC-registered portals or broker-dealers. FINRA-registered funding portals listed at [finra.org](https://www.finra.org/about/entities-we-regulate/funding-portals-we-regulate).

## 3. Grants-as-a-Service

Platforms that aggregate grant opportunities and streamline applications.

### OpenGrants

- **URL**: [https://opengrants.io](https://opengrants.io)
- **Source**: [OpenGrants Guide to Public Funding: Tech Innovation](https://guides.opengrants.io/opengrants-guide-to-public-funding/tech-innovation); [R&D Grants for Deep Tech Startups](https://opengrants.io/how-to-get-rd-grants-for-deep-tech-startups/)
- **Amount**: Varies by grant (thousands to millions). Federal R&D grants (NSF, NIH) are primary pipeline.
- **Eligibility**: Open to startups, nonprofits, researchers. Tech innovation focus includes SBIR/STTR, NSF, DOE programs.
- **Key Terms**: Decentralized platform with blockchain-based transparency. Free discovery; paid services for grant writing assistance. Covers federal, state, and private foundation grants. Strong in deep-tech and R&D categories.

### GrantStation

- **URL**: [https://www.grantstation.com](https://www.grantstation.com)
- **Source**: [Grant Management Platforms (Dynamic Business)](https://dynamicbusiness.com/featured/tech-tuesday/these-platforms-scan-thousands-of-grants-so-your-business-doesnt-have-to.html)
- **Amount**: Database of thousands of grantmakers; individual grants range from $5K to $1M+.
- **Eligibility**: Primarily nonprofits and social enterprises. Some programs open to for-profit ventures (especially SBIR/STTR listings).
- **Key Terms**: Annual subscription model with tiered pricing. Database covers private foundations, corporate givers, and government sources. Includes educational resources (tutorials, webinars) for capacity building.

### Grantable

- **URL**: [https://www.grantable.co](https://www.grantable.co)
- **Source**: [AI Grant Writing Tools 2025 (OpenGrants)](https://opengrants.io/best-ai-for-grant-writing/)
- **Amount**: N/A (writing tool, not a funder).
- **Eligibility**: Any organization applying for grants.
- **Key Terms**: AI-powered grant writing platform. Analyzes past successful proposals and generates new drafts. Particularly useful for organizations with a grant-writing history to build on.

- **Eligibility**: Contributors to the OP Stack, Ethereum infrastructure, governance tooling, developer tools. Rounds 5-7 target "everyone, everywhere putting the work in to improve Optimism."
- **Key Terms**: Retroactive model -- rewards proven impact, not promises. Badgeholder voting process. Community-driven evaluation.

## 5. AI-Specific Funding

February 2026 closed with an estimated $195B+ in tracked AI-related capital -- the most consequential month in venture finance history.

### Microsoft for Startups Founders Hub

- **URL**: [https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/startups](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/startups)
- **Source**: [Microsoft for Startups Benefits Guide 2026 (Startup Gatha)](https://startupgatha.com/microsoft-for-startups-founders-hub-benefits-credits-for-2026/); [Founders Hub Benefits (Microsoft)](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/startups/blog/founders-hub-benefits/)
- **Amount**: Up to $150,000 in Azure credits over 4 years ($1K immediately, $5K after verification, up to $150K for investor-affiliated startups). $50/month free LLM token credits for first 6 months.
- **Eligibility**: For-profit, privately held, software-based companies. Pre-Series C. Must be affiliated with an investor/accelerator in the MS Investor Network for higher tiers. No funding requirement for base tier (open to all since March 2022).
- **Key Terms**: Includes Microsoft 365 Business Premium, GitHub Enterprise, Visual Studio Enterprise seats. 24/7 Azure Standard Support. $12K in Enhanced Support credits. Up to $3K savings via Touchcast cognitive caching.

### AWS Activate

- **URL**: [https://aws.amazon.com/activate/](https://aws.amazon.com/activate/)
- **Source**: [AWS Activate Program 2026 Guide (Cloudvisor)](https://cloudvisor.co/aws-activate-program/); [AWS Startup Credits 2026 (StormIT)](https://www.stormit.cloud/blog/aws-startup-credits/)
- **Amount**: Founders Package: $1,000 credits (self-funded). Portfolio Package: up to $100,000 (provider-backed). AI startups: up to $300,000.
- **Eligibility**: Pre-Series B. Founded in last 10 years. Must have a company website. Portfolio Package requires association with an Activate Provider + their Org ID. Must not have received equal/greater credits previously.
- **Key Terms**: Credits apply to compute, storage, databases, developer tools, support plans. Typically last 12-24 months. Includes technical support and mentorship.

### NVIDIA Inception

- **URL**: [https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/startups/](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/startups/)
- **Source**: [NVIDIA Inception Guide (ThunderCompute)](https://www.thundercompute.com/blog/nvidia-inception-program-guide); [DGX Cloud Innovation Lab](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/data-center/dgx-cloud/innovation-lab/)

- **Eligibility**: Tech startups at any stage. Must employ at least one developer, have a working website, be officially incorporated, and be less than 10 years old.
- **Key Terms**: Free to join. Preferred pricing on enterprise GPUs. Access to latest SDKs, model libraries, developer tools. DGX Cloud access for early-stage AI startups. Co-marketing support.

### Google for Startups (AI Tier)

- **URL**: [https://cloud.google.com/startup/ai](https://cloud.google.com/startup/ai)

## 6. Creative Economy Funding

## 11. Government Contracts

### SAM.gov

- **URL**: [https://sam.gov](https://sam.gov)
- **Source**: [7 Critical SAM.gov Facts for 2026 (FedBiz Access)](https://fedbizaccess.com/7-essential-things-small-business-owners-must-know-about-sam-gov-in-2026/); [Government Contracting Trends 2026 (FedBiz Access)](https://fedbizaccess.com/emerging-government-contracting-trends-small-businesses-2026/)
- **Amount**: Federal contracting opportunities range from thousands to hundreds of millions.
- **Eligibility**: Any registered business. Small business certifications (8(a), WOSB, EDWOSB, HUBZone, SDVOSB) provide access to set-aside contracts.
- **Key Terms**: Registration required for all federal contracting. Certifications become inactive when registration expires. Contracting officers search SAM.gov -- expired profiles are invisible. Tech, AI, cyber, and advanced manufacturing seeing growing government demand in 2026.

### GSA Schedule (Multiple Award Schedule)

- **URL**: [https://www.gsa.gov/small-business](https://www.gsa.gov/small-business)
- **Source**: [GSA Small Business](https://www.gsa.gov/small-business); [How to Access Contract Opportunities (GSA)](https://www.gsa.gov/sell-to-government/step-1-learn-about-government-contracting/how-to-access-contract-opportunities)
- **Amount**: Varies by contract vehicle and agency need.
- **Eligibility**: Businesses with products/services that meet GSA requirements. Set-aside mechanisms at order level based on NAICS codes.
- **Key Terms**: MAS (Multiple Award Schedule) is the primary contract vehicle. Small business set-aside strategy is NAICS-dependent. Process can take 6-12 months to get on schedule.

### SBIR / STTR (Small Business Innovation Research / Technology Transfer)

- **URL**: [https://www.gsa.gov/small-business/small-business-resources/sbir-and-sbtt-programs](https://www.gsa.gov/small-business/small-business-resources/sbir-and-sbtt-programs)
- **Source**: [SBA 2025 Annual Report: Implications for 2026 (FedBiz Access)](https://fedbizaccess.com/sbas-2025-annual-report-what-it-means-for-small-business-government-contractors-heading-into-2026/); [GSA SBIR and STTR](https://www.gsa.gov/assisted-acquisition-services/acquisition-process/sbir-and-sttr)
- **Amount**: Phase I: $50K-$275K (feasibility). Phase II: $500K-$1.5M (R&D). Phase III: commercialization (no cap, funded by agencies or private sector).
- **Eligibility**: U.S. small businesses (500 or fewer employees). For-profit. Principal researcher must be employed by the company. STTR allows academic partnerships.
- **Key Terms**: 1,600+ SBIR-funded businesses reported ~$29B in commercialization activity (follow-on investment, contracts, sales). Phase III administered primarily by GSA. Non-dilutive. Highly competitive (15-25% acceptance rates). Growing momentum in AI, cyber, advanced manufacturing.
