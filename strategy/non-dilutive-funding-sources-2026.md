## 1. Government Grants

### 1.1 SBIR/STTR Programs (CRITICAL STATUS ALERT)

**URL:** https://www.sbir.gov/
**Status:** Congressional authorization EXPIRED September 30, 2025. No new solicitations or awards until reauthorization.

- Historically the largest source of early-stage non-dilutive tech funding in the U.S. (~$4B/year across 11 agencies)
- Phase I: $50K-$275K over 6-12 months (feasibility)
- Phase II: $750K-$1.8M over 24 months (development)
- Pre-expiration success rate: ~15% on average
- Monitor https://www.sbir.gov/ and https://www.grants.gov for reauthorization announcements
- Awards made before September 30, 2025 are unaffected

**Impact of expiration:** Five months without new solicitations has created a "small business innovation drought" affecting thousands of startups that relied on this pipeline. Reauthorization legislation is pending but no timeline is confirmed.

### 1.2 NSF I-Corps

**URL:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/initiatives/i-corps
**Solicitation:** NSF 25-549
**Award:** Up to $50,000 per team

- 7-week immersive entrepreneurial training program

### 1.3 NSF America's Seed Fund (NSF SBIR/STTR)

## 7. Application Best Practices

### What Reviewers Look For (2026 Landscape)

Based on practitioner guides and funder communications:

**Three core evaluation pillars:**
1. **Technical Innovation** — Clear advancement beyond current state-of-the-art; qualified team to execute
2. **Commercial/Impact Potential** — Compelling market opportunity OR measurable social impact; clear deployment pathway
3. **Funder Alignment** — How your solution directly addresses the funding agency's stated priorities and mission

**Key 2026 shifts:**
- Finance sections now carry significantly more weight; weak cost modeling is a frequent rejection reason
- Even early-stage competitions require credible pathway to deployment, adoption, or revenue
- Proposal page limits have dropped (Horizon Europe: 40 pages for RIA/IA); conciseness is rewarded
- Lump-sum grants are replacing detailed cost breakdowns in EU programs

### Common Mistakes

- **Generic proposals:** Sending the same application to multiple funders without tailoring. Reviewers detect this immediately.
- **AI-generated content:** 62% rejection rate for generic AI content; 80% for "robotic" AI content. Human voice is essential.
- **Ignoring evaluation criteria:** Reviewers score against published rubrics. Address every criterion explicitly.
- **Unrealistic timelines:** Subject matter expert reviewers flag unsupported scheduling claims.
- **Missing the "why":** Technical descriptions without clear problem statements or impact narratives.
- **Applying too broadly:** Quality over quantity. The sweet spot is 21-80 total applications with 30.89% offer rate; applying to 100+ drops success rates.

### Success Rates by Program Type

| Program | Success Rate | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| SBIR/STTR (average) | ~15% | Varies by agency; DoD higher volume, NIH more competitive |
| EU Horizon Europe (typical) | 15-30% | Varies by call and cluster |
| EIC Accelerator | <3% | Extremely competitive; fewer than 1 in 30 |
| Shuttleworth Fellowship | ~1% | Highly selective; ~100 apps per fellow |
| Creative Capital | ~5% | 109 awards from thousands of applications |
| Thiel Fellowship | ~0.1% | 20-30 from thousands |
| Awesome Foundation | ~20-30% | Varies by chapter; less competitive |

### Preparation Timeline

- **6-9 months before deadline:** Begin research on funder priorities, evaluation criteria, reviewer profiles
- **3-4 months:** Draft proposal narrative; gather letters of support
- **2 months:** Internal review; budget development; partner confirmations
- **1 month:** Final edits; compliance check; submission logistics
- **Post-submission:** Prepare for reviewer questions; begin next application

### Strategic Guidance for 2026

From [Grants.com](https://grants.com/2026-grant-funding-trends-how-small-teams-can-win-against-large-organizations/) and [24edu.info](https://www.24edu.info/business/innovation-grants-in-2026-the-funding-landscape-what-s-changed-and-how-to-win.html):

- Small teams can win against large organizations by focusing on clear, concise proposals highlighting community need and measurable impact
- Collaborate with partners to strengthen applications
- Use proven templates but customize extensively
- Seek feedback from experienced grant writers before submitting
- "Winning in 2026 is less about having a good idea and more about demonstrating mastery of the funder's logic"
