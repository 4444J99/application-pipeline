## The Product: AI Content Engine for Premium Brands

**One sentence:** Takes one premium asset (a 60s hero film, a 3D render, a product shoot) and turns it into 30 days of platform-optimized social content — automatically.

### The Problem

Premium brands pay $15-50K for a hero film or product renders. These assets get used once — posted on a website, maybe shared once on Instagram — then sit idle. Meanwhile marketing teams spend 20+ hrs/week manually creating social content and $4-8K/month on social media managers who are just manually cropping, captioning, and posting.

### What It Does

| Input | Output |
|-------|--------|
| 60s hero film | 15-20 short clips (Reels, TikTok, Stories) auto-cut at high-energy moments |
| 3D product renders | Carousel posts, comparison layouts, feature callouts |
| Brand guidelines | AI-generated captions matching brand voice, hashtag strategy |
| Posting schedule | Auto-optimized by platform, audience timezone, engagement patterns |
| Engagement data | Weekly report: reach, engagement rate, leads generated, cost-per-impression |

### Value Props (in Scott's language)
- "We turn one $30K video into 90 days of content instead of one Instagram post"
- "We save your marketing team 20 hrs/week on content creation and scheduling"
- "We increase social engagement by 40% by posting the right content at the right time"

### Who Buys
1. Scott's existing clients (MIT45, Cisco-tier) as recurring add-on to production contracts
2. Other creative agencies (white-label)
3. DTC brands spending $5-10K/month on social media managers

### Division of Labor
- Padavano: AI pipeline, scheduling automation, analytics dashboard backend
- Scott: UI/UX design, marketing, sales into his network
- Both: Use it on own socials first (build-in-public = the marketing)

### Differentiator vs Buffer/Hootsuite/Later
Those tools SCHEDULE posts. This tool CREATES the posts from premium source material. Built for brands that already have expensive content and aren't getting enough mileage from it.

### The ROI gap
None of Scott's project pages show quantitative results. This product generates those metrics — agencies stop delivering "a beautiful video" and start delivering "a content system + dashboard showing your $30K investment generated 450K impressions and 340 leads."
