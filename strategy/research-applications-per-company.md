# Research: Optimal Applications Per Company

## Consensus

**1-2 roles per company maximum.** 3 is the hard ceiling.

## Key Findings

- **2-3 applications can work** if the roles are genuinely different and each application is individually tailored. Recruiters report hiring candidates who applied to 2-3 roles.
- **Beyond 3 is counterproductive.** Recruiters rarely hired anyone who applied to 20+ roles at the same company — signals desperation and unfocused career goals.
- **ATS visibility:** Applying to 2 roles lists your resume twice in their ATS. This is fine if both are tailored. Generic resumes across multiple roles are flagged negatively.
- **Company size matters:** Large orgs with multiple recruiters handle 2 applications fine. Small companies (<50 people) — stick to 1.
- **Different channels help:** Apply through the portal for one role, LinkedIn for another. Prevents the ATS from showing a "scatter-shot" pattern.
- **Tailoring is non-negotiable.** Same resume/cover letter across multiple roles at one company is worse than not applying at all.

## What This Means for Our Pipeline

Current org-cap is set to `COMPANY_CAP = 1`. Based on this research:
- **Keep COMPANY_CAP = 1 for auto-qualify** (prevents the system from mass-promoting)
- **Allow manual override for 2nd role** when the roles are genuinely distinct
- **Never exceed 3 per company** — hard ceiling
