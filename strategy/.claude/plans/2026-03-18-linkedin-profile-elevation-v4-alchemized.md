## RESEARCH SYNTHESIS

120+ contacts analyzed across 6 archetypes. Key findings that drive every decision below:

**1. "Builder" identity transcends archetype.** The word "build" appears in nearly every exceptional profile. Profiles describing responsibilities ("manage," "oversee") read as average.

**2. The pipe-separator headline formula dominates.** `[Role] @ [Company] | [What You Build] | [Prior Signal]`

**3. Domain pivots are proof of adaptability.** BCG→FDE (Saito), astrophysics→FDE (Passaglia), lawyer→legal engineering (Taylor), Drupal→Temporal (Byron). Your MFA→systems engineering is structurally identical.

**4. Teaching + Technical is rare and always highlighted.** Mason Egger, Craig Dennis, Thomas Simonini all lead with education. You have more teaching depth (100+ courses) than any of them.

**5. Quantification is universal.** FDEs count deployments. SEs count deals. Founders count ARR. DevRel counts stars/signups. Your numbers (113/739K/2,349/23,470) already match the pattern.

**6. "Founding" signals intrapreneurship.** Reza Shabani "founded and led" an AI team. Michaela Burpoe is "Founding GTM Recruiter." The word "founding" transforms "independent" into "laboratory."

**7. Correctness language hooks Stripe/Coinbase/fintech.** "Invariants," "forward-only transitions," "zero violations," "promotion state machines" — these are the dog whistles.

**8. FDEs frame themselves as translators.** "Deploying technology on the frontlines, inside real enterprises" (Omer Khan). The bridge between product capability and customer reality.

**9. Founders post beliefs, not tasks.** "AI capability is solved; governance is the bottleneck" — contrarian beliefs > job descriptions.

**10. Lowercase/informal tone at senior level signals confidence.** Earned informality = status.
