# Disability Grant Research

Compiled from market research (strategy/what-am-i-forgetting-research-2026-03.md, Sources 9-10).

## Key Finding

Disability-specific funding is among the **least competitive** grant categories. Fewer applicants per dollar than general startup grants. Most founders don't know these programs exist.

## Programs

### Dedicated Disability Funding

| Program | Amount | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| 2Gether-International | $5M VC fund | First-ever VC fund specifically for founders with disabilities |
| Comcast | $1.5M (through 2025) | Equipping disabled startup founders |
| FedEx Entrepreneur Fund | 10 × $30,000 | For entrepreneurs with disabilities |
| PASS Program | Varies | Plan to Achieve Self-Support — set aside money for business expenses without affecting SSI |

### Broader Diversity Grants

- **169+ active diversity grants** in the US (Instrumentl database)
- Funding range: $750 — $3,000,000
- Median: $21,250 | Average: $231,520
- Categories: racial/social justice, gender equality, disability rights, LGBTQ+ advocacy, economic empowerment
- **30.6% of deadlines fall in Q2** — highest concentration of any quarter

### Critical Framing Insight

The highest-value diversity grants (averaging $231K) are not for diversity *programs* — they're for organizations **led by diverse founders** doing anything. The framing matters:

- "I'm a disabled founder building AI infrastructure" (opens doors)
- "I'm building AI infrastructure and also happen to be disabled" (does not)

Identity-first framing accesses the least competitive funding pool.
