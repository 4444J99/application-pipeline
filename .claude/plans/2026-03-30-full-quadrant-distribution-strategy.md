Every potential customer lives in one of four quadrants, defined by two axes:
- **Axis 1:** Are they an APPLICANT (seeking) or an EMPLOYER (hiring)?
- **Axis 2:** Are they an INDIVIDUAL (personal use) or an INSTITUTION (organizational use)?

## Q3: Institutional Seeker (career center, bootcamp, outplacement, government)

**How they find us:**

### Channel 11: University Career Center Partnerships
Career centers manage thousands of students applying to jobs. They currently use clunky enterprise tools (Handshake, Symplicity, 12Twenty). The pipeline engine — self-service, quality-gated, with outreach protocol — is a direct upgrade.

**Entry point:** One career center pilot. Free for 1 semester. Publish case study. Use the case study to sell to 10 more.

**Contact path:** Research career center directors at 5 universities with strong CS/engineering programs. Connect on LinkedIn. Send in-midst link (they experience the product). Follow up with pilot proposal.

### Channel 12: Bootcamp Placement Departments
Bootcamps (General Assembly, Flatiron, Springboard, Lambda) live or die by placement rates. The pipeline engine directly improves placement by adding quality gates, scoring, and outreach protocol to graduate job searches.

**Entry point:** Reach out to placement team leads. Offer the engine as open-source + support contract. Their graduates become Pro tier users.

### Channel 13: Government Employment Programs
WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act) programs spend $3.4B/year on employment services. State workforce agencies manage millions of job seekers with antiquated systems. The pipeline engine is a modernization play.

**Entry point:** Long cycle. Start with a white paper: "Applying Precision Application Management to Workforce Development." Submit to NASWA (National Association of State Workforce Agencies). Build credibility. Pilot with one state.

### Channel 14: Outplacement Firms
When companies lay off workers, they hire outplacement firms (Lee Hecht Harrison, Right Management, Challenger Gray). These firms manage hundreds of displaced workers' job searches simultaneously. The pipeline engine at scale.

**Entry point:** Contact outplacement practice leads. Pitch: "Your consultants currently manage job searches in spreadsheets. This is a state machine with quality gates." Pilot free, convert to enterprise license.

                    ├───────────┤
                    │  MICRO    │  Daily artifacts, screenshots,
                    │  CONTENT  │  metrics, shipping updates
                    └───────────┘

### Content Production Workflow

1. **Build something** (daily — the creation-first principle)
2. **Screenshot/record it** (30 seconds)
3. **Post micro-content** (daily — "shipped X, here's what it does")
4. **Synthesize into Testament post** (2/week — audit engine enforces quality)
5. **Expand into article** (1/week — Cathedral → Storefront)
6. **Compile into talk** (1/quarter — the compound interest)

The LinkedIn audit engine (built in other session) is the quality gate. Nothing ships below 6/8. The pipeline itself IS the content — every quality gate added, every bug fixed, every resume rebuilt is a story.
