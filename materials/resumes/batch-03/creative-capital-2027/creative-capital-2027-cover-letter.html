To the Creative Capital Selection Committee,

I am applying for the 2027 Creative Capital Award to support the continued expansion and public activation of ORGANVM: The Eight-Organ Metasystem. ORGANVM is an autonomous work of "Infrastructure Art" encompassing 103 repositories, 21,160 code files, and ~810K+ words of documentation, all coordinated across 8 independent GitHub organizations. It is a live, self-governing sculpture of code that treats technical governance not as a backend requirement, but as the primary medium of expression.

My work is built on the "Cathedral → Storefront" philosophy: maintaining a deep, recursive "Cathedral" of systemic logic while providing high-signal, scannable "Storefront" entry points for human-agent collaboration. In the era of frontier AI, I believe the most urgent artistic inquiry is not what we make with these models, but how we govern the systems that produce them.

The Creative Capital Award would support three catalytic innovations in my practice:

1. Governance as Formal Innovation: ORGANVM is governed by a formal promotion state machine and a validated dependency graph with 43 edges. This is a choreographic score for production. By treating the CI/CD pipeline as a performative space, verified by a corpus of 2,349+ automated tests, I am challenging the traditional boundaries of what constitutes a "finished" digital work.

2. The "AI-Conductor" Methodology: I have pioneered a methodology where human intent directs the volume of 32 specialized AI agent definitions. I have documented this process across 42 published essays (~142K words). Funding would allow me to formalize this "AI-conductor" model into a public framework, moving my practice from solo production to an institutional-scale methodology that others can inhabit.

3. Radical Transparency and Substance Proofs: My work responds to the dehumanizing opacity of automated systems. It offers a "Cathedral" built entirely on human-readable markdown and validated schemas. I aim to build the "operating system" for a more ethical digital future—one where technical integrity serves as a form of verifiable social trust.

This is a catalytic moment in my career. Over the last five years, I have built the most robust solo technical infrastructure in existence. I am now ready to activate this "Cathedral" as a public model for production, demonstrating how individual artists can reclaim institutional-scale capability through disciplined, transparent orchestration.

Thank you for your consideration of this work.

Sincerely,

Anthony James Padavano

Systems Artist & Creative Technologist | MFA, Creative Writing | New York City
