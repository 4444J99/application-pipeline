## Artist Statement

**~300 words — copy between the lines:**

---

I build creative systems — the governance IS the artwork.

My practice centers on the eight-organ system: a documented infrastructure coordinating theory, generative art, commercial products, governance, public process, community, and marketing across 113 repositories. I don't produce individual artworks. I produce the *systems* that generate, coordinate, and sustain creative work — and the visible record of how those systems are built is itself the primary creative output.

The process of creation is the product. The 48 essays I've published aren't reflections on the work; they ARE the work — the creative process rendered into prose in real time. The governance rules — registry design, dependency graphs, promotion pipelines — aren't bureaucratic overhead; they're generative constraints, the way a composer's harmonic rules shape what melodies can emerge.

I work in the tradition of solo producers who became orchestras: Brian Eno treating the studio as a compositional instrument, Trent Reznor building entire albums alone because nobody else would commit at the necessary intensity. I use AI tools the way these practitioners used their technologies — as instruments for realizing an architectural vision, not as replacements for creative judgment.

The eight-organ system is simultaneously: (a) working infrastructure that produces creative work, (b) a documented methodology for solo production at institutional scale, (c) an argument that the process of creation — made visible and governable — is itself the most interesting thing an artist can produce, and (d) a reusable model other practitioners can learn from.

**~500 words (full project description) — use for the main application field:**

---

ORGANVM is a living artwork that asks: what happens when a single artist builds an entire creative institution — not by hiring staff, but by writing the rules that make the institution run itself?

Over five years and thirty-three development sprints, I have built a system of 113 interconnected creative modules organized into eight divisions — theory, generative art, commercial tools, orchestration, public writing, community, distribution, and governance. Each division contains studios, instruments, and processes that talk to one another through formal agreements I designed. The whole system is governed by a constitution I wrote: twenty-seven specifications grounded in 130 peer-reviewed sources spanning cybernetics, institutional theory, and creative practice.

The governance structures are not administrative overhead. They are the artwork.

Think of it this way: Brian Eno treated the recording studio as a musical instrument. George Lucas built proprietary tools at Industrial Light & Magic before he could make the films he imagined. I am building the institutional machinery of a production studio — the org chart, the quality standards, the promotion pipeline, the dependency rules — as a creative act in itself. The system decides which projects are ready for public release, enforces that work flows in one direction (theory feeds art, art feeds products, never backward), and runs over a hundred automated checks to ensure nothing contradicts the constitutional principles.

This process produces three primary bodies of work. First, forty-eight published essays documenting the construction in real time — not reflections after the fact, but the creative output itself, written as the decisions are being made. These essays are public process: an artist narrating institutional design as it happens, in plain language. Second, generative artworks produced by the system's creative divisions — visual and sonic works shaped by the same governance rules that organize them. Third, the live system itself, which anyone can visit through a public portal showing the real-time state of every project, every rule, and every relationship.

I work with AI as a conductor works with an orchestra. I set the creative direction, the AI generates material at volume, and I review every note. This is not a project about artificial intelligence. It is a project about human authorship at a scale that was previously impossible for one person — and about what it means to sign your name to a body of work that required building the institution that produced it.

The system currently meets seven of seventeen maturity criteria I defined, has passed a thirty-two-day zero-incident stability test, and maintains over four thousand automated quality checks with zero rule violations. It is, by any measure, a functioning creative institution operated by one person.

The fifty thousand dollars from Creative Capital would fund three things. First, dedicated time to complete the remaining ten maturity criteria and bring the system to its first stable public release. Second, production of a curated exhibition of the generative artworks, essays, and governance visualizations — making the normally invisible architecture of institutions into something a gallery audience can see and feel. Third, documentation of the methodology so other solo artists can adapt the approach, turning the project from a personal practice into a transferable model for artist-run creative infrastructure.

ORGANVM is not a technology project that borrows art-world language. It is an art project that takes institutional design seriously enough to actually build the institution.
