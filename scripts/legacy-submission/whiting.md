## Book Proposal — "Building the Institution"

**Working title:** *Building the Institution: One Artist's Attempt to Create a Production Studio from Nothing*

**Concept:** Book-length creative nonfiction documenting the construction of ORGANVM from the inside. This is not a how-to manual for building software systems. It is a narrative of making decisions under constraint — what happens when one person, housing-precarious, working without institutional backing or inherited resources, attempts to build an institution alone. The book asks what "institution" means when stripped of its usual prerequisites: capital, staff, real estate, credentialed authority. It documents what remains when you subtract all of that and try to build anyway.

The book sits at the intersection of technology writing, creative practice documentation, and institutional critique. Its closest antecedents are Fred Turner's *From Counterculture to Cyberculture* (how technological utopianism shaped institutional forms), Ellen Ullman's *Close to the Machine* (the subjective experience of building systems under pressure), Tracy Kidder's *The Soul of a New Machine* (the narrative of a team building something ambitious under constraint), Stewart Brand's *How Buildings Learn* (how structures evolve through use rather than design intent), and Robin Sloan's work at the boundary of creative practice and technological infrastructure. The difference is that *Building the Institution* is written from inside the construction, in real time, by the person doing the building.

**Structure:**

- **Part I: Foundation** — Why build this. What precarity demands and what it forecloses. The decision to treat constraint as a design parameter rather than an obstacle. Living at your mother's house at 35 and building anyway.
- **Part II: Architecture** — The eight-organ model. How governance becomes creative medium. Why a system that coordinates theory, art, commerce, and community through automated rules is itself an artwork.
- **Part III: Constitutional** — Grounding the system in 130 peer-reviewed academic sources across 27 constitutional specs. Why an artist's studio needs a constitution. What it means to treat institutional design as a scholarly and creative discipline simultaneously.
- **Part IV: Scale** — The AI-conductor methodology. 4,000+ automated tests, 104 CI workflows, 50 dependency edges, 7/17 maturity criteria met. What one person can build when automation is treated as an instrument rather than a replacement. The 32-day soak test. The stakeholder portal. The live system.
- **Part V: Exhibition** — Making invisible systems visible. Governance as performance. The problem of presenting institutional infrastructure as art to audiences trained to expect objects.

**Current material:** 48 published essays totaling 200,000+ words serve as both source material and rough drafts. The book does not start from nothing — it synthesizes and structures what the essays have been documenting in real time since the project's inception. The essays are the field notes; the book is the expedition account.

**Why the Whiting Foundation:** The Whiting Creative Nonfiction Grant funds writers completing ambitious book-length nonfiction projects. This book is already being written — the 48 essays constitute a sustained, public, real-time draft of its core arguments. What the grant provides is dedicated time to structure, revise, and complete the manuscript: to move from accumulated field documentation to a shaped narrative that can reach readers beyond the project's existing audience.
