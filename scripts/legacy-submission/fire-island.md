## Project Description / Residency Proposal

**~375 words — copy between the lines:**

---

**Proposed project: "The Governance Séance" — participatory performance and essay series**

Cherry Grove has a century of queer people inventing the rules for how to live together — who gets the house, who holds the lease, who stays through winter. These are governance questions. My practice is building governance structures for creative work. At Fire Island I want to bring those two things into the same room.

The project has three concrete outputs:

**1. The Governance Séance (3 participatory performances)**
Structured events where the ORGANVM system's actual governance processes — promoting a repository from CANDIDATE to PUBLIC status, auditing a dependency edge, voting on whether a new organ gets created — are performed live with the residency community as participants. The audience does not watch a simulation. They make real decisions about a real system. Each séance uses a different governance rule as its dramaturgical spine: the first follows a promotion (who decides when work is ready?), the second follows a dependency audit (what are you allowed to need from someone else?), the third follows a community vote on system expansion (who gets to be part of the architecture?). These questions resonate differently in a queer community that has always had to build its own institutions from scratch.

**2. Fire Island Essays (4-6 new entries in the public process series)**
Weekly essays written and published during the residency, documenting what happens when a digital infrastructure project enters physical community space. The tension between systems and embodiment. What governance feels like when the nodes are people in the room, not repositories on a screen. These join the existing 48-essay archive — the residency becomes part of the system's permanent public record.

**3. "Build Your Own Organ" Workshop (1-2 sessions, open to community)**
A workshop where residents and Cherry Grove community members design governance structures for their own creative practices, using the eight-organ model as a starting template. Not a tech tutorial — a conversation about how you organize a life's work when no institution is going to do it for you. Designed for artists, writers, and organizers who are building without a net.

The residency community becomes ORGAN-VI — the community organ — for real. Not metaphorically. The decisions made at Fire Island enter the system.
