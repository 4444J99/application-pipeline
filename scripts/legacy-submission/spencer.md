## Research Proposal — Education Research

**Research Question:** How do formally documented governance systems, designed for creative practice and grounded in peer-reviewed literature, function as pedagogical tools for teaching systems thinking, institutional design, and AI literacy?

**Significance:** Most existing research on AI in education focuses on AI as a content delivery mechanism — tutoring, grading, personalized learning paths. This study examines a fundamentally different pedagogical frame: AI as a collaborative instrument for institutional design. The ORGANVM system represents a documented case study in which one practitioner used AI-conductor methodology (human directs, AI generates volume, human reviews) to build and govern a 113-repository institutional infrastructure grounded in 130 peer-reviewed sources. The constitutional corpus — 27 specs totaling 84,000+ words of grounding text — constitutes a teaching artifact unlike anything currently documented in education research. This study asks whether such formally governed creative systems can serve as pedagogical tools for teaching the meta-skills that matter most in an AI-augmented world: systems thinking, institutional design, and critical AI literacy.

**Methodology:** Mixed methods across four phases:

1. **Content analysis** of the 27 constitutional specs and 48 published essays as a case study in documented institutional design. How does the governance structure (promotion state machines, dependency validation, 50 tracked edges with zero violations) function as a teaching text? What systems-thinking competencies does engagement with the material develop?

2. **Methodology analysis** of the AI-conductor model as a teaching framework. The system's 4,000+ automated tests, 104 CI workflows, and 32-day zero-incident soak test provide empirical evidence of the methodology's rigor. How can this model be adapted for classroom use?

3. **Educator interviews** with 5-10 educators across creative technology, systems design, and AI ethics who engage with the system documentation. Semi-structured interviews exploring how they interpret the governance artifacts, what pedagogical affordances they identify, and what barriers they encounter.

4. **Toolkit development** — "Build Your Own Organ," a pedagogical toolkit enabling students to apply governance-as-creative-medium principles to their own multi-project creative practices. Includes workshop curriculum, template governance documents, and a simplified seed.yaml contract system.

**Expected Outputs:**
- Peer-reviewed paper submitted to a journal such as *Computers & Education* or *International Journal of Art & Design Education*
- "Build Your Own Organ" pedagogical toolkit (open-source, Creative Commons licensed)
- Workshop curriculum (tested in at least one pilot session)

**Timeline (12 months):**
- Months 1-3: Content analysis of constitutional corpus and essay archive
- Months 4-6: AI-conductor methodology documentation and pedagogical framework development
- Months 7-9: Educator interviews, data analysis, toolkit prototyping
- Months 10-12: Toolkit refinement, paper writing, workshop pilot

**Researcher Qualifications:** While I hold an independent researcher position rather than a university affiliation, the research capacity is demonstrated by the work itself: 11 years of college teaching across 8+ institutions (2,000+ students, 100+ courses) constitutes substantial pedagogical experience. The system's 27 constitutional specs grounded in 130 peer-reviewed sources demonstrate scholarly rigor. The 48 published essays constitute a longitudinal documentation practice. The 4,000+ automated tests and live stakeholder portal demonstrate methodological verification capacity. This is not a proposal to build something — it is a proposal to study something that already exists and is already documented at a depth that most research projects aspire to.
